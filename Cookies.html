Kompare AI – Cookie Policy

Effective Date: December 4, 2025

1. What Are Cookies?

Cookies are small text files stored on your device (computer, phone, or tablet) by your browser to improve your experience, remember your preferences, and enable core functionalities of a website or application.

2. Types of Cookies We Use

2.1 Essential / Functional Cookies

These cookies are crucial for the basic operation of Kompare AI. They:

  • Keep you logged in
  • Maintain user sessions
  • Secure authentication

Note: These cannot be disabled for core functionality. Disabling them will prevent you from logging in and using the service.

2.2 Analytics Cookies

These cookies help us understand how users interact with our platform, allowing us to measure and improve Kompare AI. We use tools like:

  • Google Analytics
  • PostHog
  • Mixpanel

These cookies track user behavior anonymously to generate aggregated statistics. **We deploy these cookies only after receiving your consent via our cookie banner or preference center.**

2.3 Advertising Cookies (Future Use)

We may add advertising cookies in the future. If implemented, they may be used for:

  • Showing relevant offers and promotions
  • Personalizing product recommendations
  • Retargeting users with relevant ads

These cookies will be optional, and **will only be deployed with your explicit, separate consent.**

3. Third-Party Cookies

We use certain third-party services that may place their own cookies on your device. These may include:

  • Google OAuth (for sign-in)
  • Stripe / Razorpay (payment processing)
  • Supabase (backend services)
  • Analytics platforms (as listed above)

4. Managing Cookies

You have the ability to manage or disable cookies through your web browser settings. The steps vary depending on your browser (e.g., Chrome, Firefox, Safari).

Warning: Disabling essential cookies may break login and other core functionalities of Kompare AI, preventing you from using the service effectively.

5. Updates to Cookie Policy

We may update this Policy regularly to reflect changes in our practices or legal requirements. We encourage you to review this page periodically.
